# Multi-Tenant SaaS Identity (B2B)

## When to Choose This Pattern

Choose this pattern if your application is a B2B SaaS product and you need a reliable identity and access management (IAM) foundation from day one.

A B2B SaaS product is software that one business provides to other businesses through the cloud. Each customer business expects separate users, administrators, policies, and branding. If your product serves multiple customer companies and each company needs its own workspace, your product follows a B2B SaaS model.

Thunder helps you solve the IAM challenges in that model. You can onboard organizations, control access, connect enterprise identity providers, and apply security and governance controls without building each capability from scratch.

## Use Case Scope

From a B2B application owner view, you need to solve the full customer lifecycle, from signup to security, governance, and growth reporting. Thunder provides capabilities for each stage.

### Organization Onboarding (Signup)

As the first problem, you define how a new customer company subscribes to your application. In this stage, one user acts on behalf of a business and completes two mandatory actions in one flow:

1. Register the user in the platform.
2. Create a dedicated workspace for that user and assign that user as the workspace owner.

Thunder supports flexible signup models that you can align with your product journey:

- Username, password, and other user profile attributes.
- Google authentication.
- GitHub authentication.
- Microsoft authentication.

Thunder also supports administrator verification during signup by using:

- Email OTP.
- SMS OTP.
- Magic link.

A visitor can sign up as an **Organization Admin** to create a new workspace (organization). Thunder manages admin users at the root-level organizational unit (OU).

For operational integration needs, Thunder exposes organization lifecycle events, including workspace creation status, so your internal systems and third-party systems can synchronize and provision related data.

If your registration flow includes commercial controls, Thunder helps you integrate subscription models before workspace onboarding completes, including:

- Billing service integration.
- Business verification service integration.

### Collaboration

After onboarding, you need each customer company to grow safely inside its own workspace. Thunder lets organization admins invite users to collaborate in the workspace. If your business model allows it, invited users can create new workspaces.

### Organizational Identity Management

As your customers scale, identity operations become a daily requirement. Thunder lets you onboard users directly, invite users, connect bring your own identity provider (BYOIDP), use just-in-time (JIT) provisioning, and support account linking. Thunder also supports user store configuration for advanced edge cases.

### Identity Recovery

When users lose access, fast recovery reduces support cost and user frustration. Thunder provides username recovery and password recovery flows to restore access securely.

### Account and Access Administration

You need access controls that match organization policy and risk posture. Thunder provides account locking, user grouping, and role assignment so you can enforce least-privilege access across customer workspaces.

### Organizational Branding

Enterprise customers expect workspace-level branding and access URL control. Thunder lets you override allowed user interface branding elements for each organization, define an organization subdomain, and plan for fully custom domains in phase 2.

### Organization Discovery Mechanisms

In multi-organization products, users need a reliable way to reach the correct organization context. Thunder supports organization discovery by organization ID for direct child organization access. Thunder also supports email-domain discovery in phase 2 and extensibility to plug in custom discovery mechanisms in phase 2.

### Organizational Sign-In and Authentication

Some B2B products use one global sign-in flow, while others need organization-specific controls. Thunder supports both models. You can use the globally defined sign-in flow and alter login options based on the capabilities allowed for each organization.

### Security and Governance

B2B customers expect strong controls by default. Thunder provides security and privacy capabilities that you can apply at organization level.

Security capabilities include:

- Account locking.
- CAPTCHA.
- Rate limiting.

Privacy and compliance capabilities include:

- General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) support.
- Consent management.

### Troubleshooting

When a customer reports an access issue, you need organization-level diagnostic visibility. Thunder provides logs that help your teams troubleshoot organization-level issues quickly.

### Insights

To run a SaaS business, you need usage and risk insights per customer organization. Thunder provides organization insights, including:

- Monthly active users (MAU).
- Inactive users.
- Locked users.
- Failed login attempts.
